    ENGINEERING OF THE KNIT

    THERMAL MASS

    We approached the beanie not as an accessory, but as an architectural insulator. By analyzing thermal retention properties of heavy-gauge yarns, we developed a knit structure that creates micro-pockets of trapped air, acting as an impenetrable barrier against harsh environmental conditions while maintaining breathability.

    STRUCTURAL SOFTNESS

    The challenge of monolithic design in knitwear is balancing rigid form with necessary flexibility. Through advanced tension mapping during the weaving process, we achieved a silhouette that holds its structural integrity—a clean, brutalist line—while providing absolute comfort against the skin.
